How I became a Web Developer in New York City without even speaking the language

After many years of living in New York City, and working in the restaurant industry my constant love for technology guide me into the path of becoming a web developer, aginst all odds and obstacles I am living proof that if you are devoted, dedicated and constant, you can become whatever you want as long as you follow your heart, in my case my heart is in technology.

Curiosity

I have always been very curious about how things work in general, reason why I tend to take apart my computers, put them back together (sometimes I break them), set up sound systems, and in general I believe anyone can do anything as long as they want to learn, yes I am the kind of person that reads the manual.

Tech Made Me Successful

After a couple of years of working in a restaurant (learning the language), I became the manager, I instantly started adding technology to the place, I added a timetrax machine to control personal schedule, IP cameras for security and an app to be able to watch them from anywhere, a POS system for quicker service, a website, online ordering, automated invoice management, a new computer and an iPad to be able to work and keep moving, every change was followed by revenue growth, I did everything with close to zero budget, even if I hat to wire things my self (I actually did) so my bosses loved me...

Code Changed My Life

I decided to go back to school, I wanted a challenge and a change. While taking classes at NYU a programming professor told me I should look into coding, It seemed like it came easy to me, and it did!, I really enjoyed coding, I took an MIT computer science class and after that all I wanted to do was to code, so I started teaching my self how to do it.

Coding is My Passion

Finally, I quit my job, and dedicated all my time to learn to code and become great at it, after a while I started learning more and more, I have participated in hackathons and almost won Google's Devfest 2016 (3rd place... so close). Recently I have been teaching code at a High school in Brooklyn and learning to combine code with design, I am a full stack web developer and want to be part of a company that feels the same way about technology as I do.

I believe every kid should learn to code at an early age since they are the ones who are going to mold the future of humanity.
